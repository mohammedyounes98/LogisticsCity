Peri-urban Logisitcs Studies

Analysis of the warehousing differential prices between urban and suburban areas

The Logistics City Chair is dedicated to research on urban logistics, with a focus on three scientific axes:

THEME 1. Urban logistics real estate , new economic models of metropolitan logistics real estate, strategies for implementing logistics buildings in large metropolises.

You can find all the documents relating to the Chair’s warehouse database and cartographic production on a dedicated page accessible in the following tab “Warehouse database” .

GOALS :

  • Logistics sprawl and urban logistics : analysis of territorial dynamics linked to the evolution of the location of logistics activities, at the “macro” level.

  • Database on warehouse location logic in 78 world cities , following a comparative meta-analysis.

    • The summary presentation of the updated research (October 2023) can be discovered via this link .
    • The research report (September 2023) co-written by Renata de Oliveira and Matthieu Schorung, under the supervision of Laetitia Dablanc is available via this link .
    • The entire Chair database can be consulted via this link . The dataset on logistics sprawl can be consulted here .
    • You can find the research report (2022) on logistics sprawl and the price differential for warehouse rental between urban and peri-urban areas based on a comparison of several dozen global metropolises, co-written by Renata de Oliveira, Matthieu Schorung and Laetitia Dablanc.
    • The initial presentation at the launch of this research, by Laetitia Dablanc, Laura Palacios-Argüello and Leise de Oliveira (2020), on the logic of warehouse location is still available via this link .

  • Logistics urban planning : analysis of public policies, the history of the production of the first buildings, the economic paradox of urban logistics and regulatory obstacles, at the “micro” level.
